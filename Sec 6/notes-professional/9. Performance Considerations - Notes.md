# Notes: Performance Considerations (Professional)

*From `9. Performance Considerations.txt`.*

---

## 1. Overhead can dominate

Parallel loops introduce overhead (partitioning, scheduling, synchronization, context switching, cache effects). For small per-iteration work (e.g. simple arithmetic), the sequential loop may outperform `Parallel.For`.

---

## 2. When parallel loops are appropriate

Parallel loops tend to be beneficial when:

- the iteration count is very large, and/or
- each iteration performs substantial work (CPU-bound, longer duration)

---

## 3. Measure performance empirically

The video demonstrates measuring elapsed time around the loop and comparing sequential vs parallel implementations; this is the recommended approach rather than assuming parallel is faster.

---

## Summary table

| Scenario | Guidance |
|---------|----------|
| Light work per iteration | Sequential often faster. |
| Heavy work or huge range | Parallel can win. |
| Uncertain | Benchmark with representative data. |
